<objective>
Resolve THE one correctness risk this phase's design carries (D-05 / 13-PATTERNS.md CONFIRMED FINDING):
`bump_memory_hit` refreshes hit_count/last_seen_at/salience but NEVER `expires_at`, so a still-true
"steady favorite" that re-distills to a near-duplicate each cycle is silently no-op'd by the 0.92
dedup gate and ages past its (now shorter, 30-day) horizon while the user keeps being true to that
taste. Fix it by making `remember()` kind-aware in two places: insert with the per-kind decay horizon
(D-03), and — for short-decay kinds only — refresh `expires_at` on the dedup-hit (self-refresh, D-05),
using the `refresh_memory_expiry` helper from plan 13-02 and the `resolve_decay_days` resolver from
plan 13-01.

Purpose: TASTE-02 requires taste episodes to decay on their own tier, and D-05's self-refresh is what
makes a low-salience + short-decay taste memory safe (durable taste survives by staying true and being
re-written, not by never expiring). The change MUST leave Phase 11 kinds byte-identical — they are
absent from MEMORY_DECAY_DAYS_BY_KIND, so they keep the 90-day insert horizon and never refresh.
Output: two surgical edits to `services/memory.py::remember`; new `tests/test_memory_taste.py`.
</objective>
